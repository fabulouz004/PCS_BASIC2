PERIOD 2
GRAMMATICAL ACCURACY (IRREGULAR NOUNS)

Irregular nouns are nouns that form their plural by adding ‘es', ‘ies', ‘bes’, i.e; words that end in ‘y', in their plural form the ‘y' will be thrown away and be replaced with ‘ies' while those that end with ‘s' will be replaced with ‘ves’ but when a vowel comes immediately before the ‘y' or before ‘f' the ‘y' or ‘f' is not thrown away, but an ‘s' will be added to make it plural.

Example
Singular Plural
Baby Babies
lady ladies
lorry lorries
company companies
knife knives
calf calves
Shelf Shelves
Wife Wives
Loaf Loaves
Leaf Leaves
Half Halves
Wolf Wolves
Boy Boys
Toy Toys
Key Keys
But there
are exceptions.
Chief Chiefs
Sheriff Sheriffs
Beef Beefs
Roof Roofs.
